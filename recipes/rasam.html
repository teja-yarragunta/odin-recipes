Authentic Rasam Recipe

Description

Rasam is a traditional South Indian soup known for its tangy, spicy, and aromatic flavors. It is typically made with tamarind, tomatoes, and a blend of spices, often served as a comforting accompaniment to rice.

This rasam recipe brings authentic flavors to your table, perfect for a light, healthy, and warming meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups water
  • 1 small lemon-sized ball of tamarind (soaked and extracted)
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup cooked toor dal (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons rasam powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 2 teaspoons ghee or oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • Chopped coriander leaves for garnish
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water. Add the tamarind extract, chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, and salt.
  2. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the mixture becomes fragrant.
  3. Add rasam powder and black pepper powder. Mix well.
  4. If using cooked toor dal, mash it well and add to the mixture. Simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Heat ghee or oil in a separate pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  6. Add dried red chilies, curry leaves, crushed garlic, and asafoetida. Fry until aromatic.
  7. Pour this tempering over the rasam and mix well.
  8. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice or enjoy it as a flavorful soup.
